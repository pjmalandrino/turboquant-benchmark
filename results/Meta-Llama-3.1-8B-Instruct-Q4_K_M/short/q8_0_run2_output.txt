Explain how CRDTs (Conflict-free Replicated Data Types) work for real-time collaborative text editing. Cover the difference between operation-based and state-based approaches, and explain why Yjs is commonly chosen over Automerge for production editors. 

## Step 1: Understanding CRDTs
CRDTs (Conflict-free Replicated Data Types) are a type of data structure designed to handle concurrent updates in a distributed system. They ensure that the data remains consistent even in the presence of conflicts or concurrent modifications. In the context of real-time collaborative text editing, CRDTs are used to maintain a shared document state across multiple users.

## Step 2: Operation-based vs State-based CRDTs
There are two primary approaches to implementing CRDTs: operation-based and state-based. Operation-based CRDTs track the sequence of operations (e.g., insertions, deletions) applied to the data, while state-based CRDTs maintain the current state of the data directly.

## Step 3: Operation-based CRDTs
Operation-based CRDTs, such as those used in Yjs, track the sequence of operations applied to the document. Each operation is assigned a unique ID, and the CRDT ensures that the operations are applied in the correct order, even in the presence of concurrent modifications. This approach allows for efficient conflict resolution and ensures that the document remains consistent.

## Step 4: State-based CRDTs
State-based CRDTs, such as those used in Automerge, maintain the current state of the document directly. They use a combination of vector clocks and merge functions to resolve conflicts and ensure consistency. While state-based CRDTs can be more efficient in some cases, they can be more complex to implement and may require more computational resources.

## Step 5: Why Yjs is commonly chosen over Automerge
Yjs is commonly chosen over Automerge for production editors due to its operation-based approach, which provides several advantages. Firstly, Yjs is more efficient in terms of computational resources, making it suitable for real-time collaborative editing. Secondly, Yjs's operation-based approach allows for more fine-grained control over conflict resolution, making it easier to implement custom conflict resolution strategies. Finally, Yjs has a more extensive community and ecosystem, making it easier to find resources and support for implementing Yjs in production editors.

## Step 6: Conclusion
In conclusion, CRDTs are a crucial component of real-time collaborative text editing, ensuring that the shared document state remains consistent even in the presence of conflicts or concurrent modifications. While both operation-based and state-based CRDTs have their advantages, Yjs's operation-based approach makes it a popular choice for production editors due to its efficiency, flexibility, and extensive community support.
